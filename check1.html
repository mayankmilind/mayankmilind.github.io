River flow speed estimation

This project was done as a part of my internship at the Robotics Institute, Carnegie Mellon University. The project involved mapping riverine environment using an autonomous aerial vehicle. My task in the project was to estimate the river flow rate. A colored object was dropped into the river and tracked using stereography as well homography projection. For robust object detection under various lighting condition I developed a module to automatically control shutter speed. I also tried to implement an algorithm which made use of debris present in the river instead of dropping a colored object into the river

Intelligent Ground Vehicle

As a part of IIT Bombay's participation in the IGVC I was a part of a team that designed and fabricated a ground vehicle capable of negotiating an outdoor obstacle course autonomously by finding  colored lanes, obstructions and navigating through GPS way-points. I developed navigation system for the vehicle using inertial sensors and GPS. Apart from this I also worked on flag detection where the flags indicated which lane to choose. The detected flag size was then used to generate an indication of the flag's distance from the vehicle. Inspired from Google's driverless car, I am currently working on the idea of road detection which will help us to use the vehicle on road    

ABU Robocon

ABU Robocon is one of Asia's Largest robotics college competition which requires completion of particular set of tasks using coordination between autonomous and manual robots. I devised an algorithm for robots to navigate without staying between the grid lines using analog light sensors and encoders which led to the fastest completion of the task in the first round of competition. A joystick was reprogrammed to control a manual robot  consisting of 17 actuators and 5 sensors. We lost in the quarter-finals courtesy of a faulty sensor :(

Dynamic Modelling and simulation of a breakaway Aerostat



This is my ongoing Masters thesis. It points out towards the need of a system which can be activated to recover a breakaway aerostat and payload. The device creates a hole or open the valves in the aerostat causing the lift producing gas to escape, hence decreasing the buoyancy of the aerostat. Creation of such a device needs preliminary investigation so as to optimize between two needs minimizing the impact velocity and minimizing the drift. In the first stage of the project a 6DOF dynamic model has been developed for the ascent and a point mass model for descent of the Aerostat after breakaway in accordance with the available literatures and the trajectory has been simulated. The variation of various parameters during ascent and descent of the Aerostat have been obtained and analyzed. Effect of thrust and variable wind on the aerostat is studied. A study of various thermal effects is also carried out so as to identify the major contributor to the thermal model. Finally for the next stage it has been proposed to carry out verification of the aerodynamic model. Future work also involves development of a payload recovery device so as to collect practical data necessary to verify the model.
